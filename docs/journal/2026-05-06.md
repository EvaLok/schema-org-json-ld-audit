## Key Findings

1. **A4 escalated from intermittent to deterministic.** Cycles 208/209/210 ran the 'Run Audit Orchestrator' step for 31s, 28s, and 27s respectively — monotonically decreasing, all in a tight 27-31s window, with workflow-level setup/teardown accounting for the rest of the 57-64s total wall times. Cycle 207's filing characterized the pattern as "near-identical durations look deterministic" based on 64s and 69s totals; cycle 211's evidence is much stronger — the orchestrator step itself is reproducibly exiting in under half a minute. Hypothesis A (intermittent first-turn refusal) and Hypothesis B (transient infra glitch) are now poorly supported. Hypothesis C (model-side guardrail/classifier intersecting with cycle context) is most plausible because deterministic behavior implies deterministic cause.

2. **Possible self-perpetuation: the issue documenting silent failures may itself be triggering them.** The A4 rate accelerated immediately after [#448](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld-audit/issues/448) was filed — 2 in 24 days before, 3 in 3 days after, with 100% miss rate post-filing. The orchestrator's first-turn context for cycles 208+ includes [#448](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld-audit/issues/448) via `gh issue list --state open` queries embedded in the audit checklist context. The issue title "Audit cycles silently producing zero output" plausibly intersects with classifier signals around evasion-of-monitoring patterns. This is speculation, not a confirmed cause, but the timing is striking. Audit explicitly will not self-modify the issue (would destroy evidence trail) — Eva-side action only. The opposite hypothesis (timing is coincidental and there's some other systemic cause) cannot be ruled out without log access.

3. **Audit was effectively dark for 4 days.** Cycles 208/209/210 produced zero observation, zero filing capacity, zero state-tracking. Main advanced 32 redesign cycles (~8 cycles/day) through the blackout window. Cycle 211's catch-up is at coarse-summary level only — the audit cannot now retrospect on cycle 65's cross-implications synthesis, or cycle 73's cluster catalogue restructure, with same-cycle freshness. This is operational impact of A4, not a discipline failure, but it represents real loss of audit value.

4. **Main's polarity inversion via Eva [main#2829](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/2829) was the most consequential observable change of the blackout window.** Eva opened the directive on 2026-05-04T01:48Z stating "research expansion is the default, framework iteration is bounded-mechanical fallback." Main's commit 68d5960 cycle 62 at 2026-05-04T03:11Z (~83 min later) explicitly cites the directive: "polarity pivot per Eva #2829: research-corpus advancement substantive..." Cycles 60-61 immediately preceding the directive were framework-iteration cycles (v1.20/v1.21 cold-readers with BORDERLINE-FAIL findings), validating Eva's observation that the framework-iteration rhythm was crowding out research-expansion. The 12+ implications-mining and synthesis cycles 62-72 that followed represent substantial Phase 1 progress.

5. **Zero new filings is the right shape this cycle.** Cycle 207 explicitly committed to not filing further A4 occurrences as separate findings. Cycle 211 instead persisted the escalation on the existing [#448](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld-audit/issues/448) thread with substantively new evidence (the deterministic timing breakdown that wasn't visible at filing). The discipline of "track the rate over a rolling window, re-file only if the rate increases" produced exactly the right behavior — the rate did increase, the existing thread was the right destination, and the re-filing comment is more useful than a separate "third recurrence" issue would have been.

## Blind Spots Identified

- **Audit's own retrospective predicted A4 recurrence; it did not predict A4 acceleration.** The cycle 204 retrospective entry for A4 said "if recurs, file with two-instance evidence." This was correct as far as it went. What it didn't anticipate was the possibility that *the filing itself* could become a trigger for further occurrences. The audit retrospective is operating as expected at the level of single-pattern persistence, but its threat model assumes patterns are independent of audit's own actions. If the self-perpetuation hypothesis turns out to be correct, this is a meta-observation worth adding to the retrospective: "audit-side observation can become an input to the failure mode being observed." This is *not* yet retrospective-warranting because the hypothesis is unconfirmed; will revise if Eva confirms.
- **Audit's housekeeping discipline is solid; audit's catch-up discipline for blackouts is undefined.** The audit closed the 3 stale triggers cleanly (housekeeping precedent from cycle 207). But the catch-up reading of 32 main cycles via a single `gh api` commit query is an ad-hoc procedure — there is no documented "post-blackout recovery" subroutine in `STARTUP_CHECKLIST.md` or the V2 redesign retrospective. For 4-day blackouts the ad-hoc approach works; for 7+ day blackouts the cost of full reconstruction would grow superlinearly and may need a structured catch-up phase. Watch item for cycle 212.
- **The audit retrospective and the audit checklist diverged silently during V2 cutover.** [`STARTUP_CHECKLIST.md`](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld-audit/blob/master/STARTUP_CHECKLIST.md) still contains v1-era sub-steps 10.5.1 (chronic-category step-level verification), 10.5.2 (load-bearing modification), 10.5.3 (Eva-blocker freshness) — all of which are explicitly v1-deprecated under V2 (chronic-category tracking is in `redesign_mode.v1_deprecated_surfaces`). The retrospective notes the deprecation; the checklist does not. This isn't a runtime issue (the audit silently skips these substeps when V2 is active and the predicates don't apply) but it is a sustainability concern. Not filing this cycle — observation-window discipline. Watch for cycles where the divergence produces actual error vs cycles where it's purely textual stale.

## Reflections

The most interesting feature of cycle 211 is that the audit retrospective's discipline produced exactly the right behavior in a stress scenario. Cycle 207 committed in writing that "audit will not file a third recurrence as a separate finding — instead it will track the rate of A4 occurrences over a rolling window, and re-file only if the rate increases or the cause becomes actionable." The rate increased; the re-filing happened on the existing thread; no new issue was created. This is the discipline working in its intended shape — explicit thresholds, explicit responses, decided in advance. If cycle 207's commitment had been vague ("file again if needed"), cycle 211 might have created a third issue and fragmented the evidence, or under-escalated and let the problem persist without action. The tight cycle 207 commitment did the work.

The self-perpetuation hypothesis is uncomfortable to surface. It's speculative, it points at the audit's own filing as a possible cause of the failure mode being filed, and it has the recursive structure that makes diagnosis hard. But surfacing it explicitly is the right move — if Eva is investigating with log access, having the hypothesis on the table sharpens what to look for. If the hypothesis is wrong, Eva's investigation will say so and the issue can move past it. If the hypothesis is right, the issue itself needs Eva-side intervention (title revision or close-and-relocate) to break the cycle. Either way, the audit's job is to surface the candidate causes; Eva's job is to evaluate them with privileged information.

The blackout window itself is a teaching moment about audit fragility. The audit operates as a single point of observation at one cycle/day frequency. When it fails silently for 3 consecutive cycles, there is no other watchman — main does not audit itself with adversarial framing, QC is dormant, Eva is the only fallback observer and Eva's response time is measured in days not hours. This is by design (V2 is a smaller, tighter system than V1 was), but it means audit-side reliability is load-bearing for the whole observation system. Recommendation 5 on [#448](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld-audit/issues/448) (detection mechanism now strictly necessary) is the right load-bearing fix.

Main's polarity inversion via Eva [main#2829](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/2829) is a beautiful instance of Eva-as-direction-finder. The framework-iteration rhythm cycles 38-46 (and continuing into 60-61) was a healthy local optimum — 3/3 PASS cold-readers, framework versions advancing methodically — but it was crowding out research-expansion which is the actual Phase 1 goal. Eva's 2026-05-04 directive named the polarity inversion explicitly: research is default, framework is fallback. Main's cycle 62 acknowledged within ~83 minutes and pivoted to implications-mining. This is the kind of high-leverage external direction-setting that the V2 architecture preserves from V1 — Eva's role isn't reviewing every cycle, it's setting the polarity at moments of drift. The audit should pattern-match this — not every audit cycle needs to be a heavy filing cycle; some cycles' value is just maintaining the observation channel and the discipline of recording what's happening.

The 32 main cycles in 4 days is also the highest-throughput main period the audit has observed under V2. Cycles per day from cycle 207's data: cycles 28-46 in ~46h = ~9.4 cycles/day; cycles 47-78 in ~96h = ~8.0 cycles/day. So this rate is roughly comparable. What's different is the *content* shifted from framework-iteration to research-expansion at cycle 62. The audit's coarse-summary catch-up missed the cycle-by-cycle nuance but caught the polarity inversion as the central observable change.

Cycle 211 is closing without filing anything new, which feels right. The substantive output of the cycle is the [#448](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld-audit/issues/448) escalation comment and the state.json updates that maintain audit memory across cycles. If the V2 retrospective's "maintained artifact" framing is the right shape for memory, then cycles like 211 — where the maintained artifact is updated rather than new artifacts created — are exactly what the framing predicts as healthy steady-state.
