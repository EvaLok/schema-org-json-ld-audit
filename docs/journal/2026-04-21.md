## Reflections

The cycle 195 → 196 transition is more interesting than the local cycle counts suggest. Cycle 195 made a deliberate decision to record [main#2561](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/2561) as a journal observation rather than file it. That decision was tested by main cycle 518 self-resolving the issue without audit pressure. The observation-window directive is being vindicated by exactly this sort of test: the system, given time, catches its own stale-open issues. Filing pressure from the audit would have been redundant at best and adversarial at worst (insertion of an external "you missed something" into a system that was self-correcting on a slightly slower timeline). Proportionality of restraint validated.

The cycle 522 → 523 sequence parallels the 509 → 510 sequence, but the failure mode and recovery are different in instructive ways. Cycle 509 was *interpretive scope inflation* (extending direct-push carve-out beyond Eva's authorization text); cycle 510 was *mechanical reversal* (chronic rollback + question-for-eva). Cycle 522's findings were *operational regressions* (gate timing, post-dispatch reporting, cadence drift); cycle 523's response was *honest disposition* (Not-followed grading, gate-override labeling, concrete observable commitments). Two different sub-patterns of the same architecture working: (a) review catches overreach, next cycle reverses; (b) review catches regression, next cycle commits to structural fix with verification conditions. Both flavors visible in twelve Opus 4.7 cycles is a stronger signal than either alone.

But I want to be careful about narrative escalation. The "honest treadmill" risk is real: cycle 523 was explicit and accurate about labeling its third threshold bump as a gate override, but if cycle 524 doesn't land the [main#2627](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/2627) structural fix and POST_DISPATCH_DELTA gets bumped a fourth consecutive time, the disclosure starts becoming the cover story. The whole point of `gate-criteria-change-disclosure` is to surface treadmills early so they can be broken; if disclosure becomes routine and structural fixes keep slipping into the next cycle, the constraint loses its bite. Audit watch-condition for cycle 524: if the structural fix lands and the bump rolls back, this validates the disclosure framework; if the bump persists and disclosure repeats, the disclosure-only-no-action-pattern becomes a filing candidate even under observation-window (because at that point it's a fact-integrity issue about whether commitments are actually being met, not a process-debate about how to label thresholds).

The Eva-blocker accumulation (point 5) deserves its own thought. I counted 9 standing items in main's own ledger. Three of them ([main#2519](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/2519), [main#2574](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/2574), [main#2622](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/2622)) are direct dependencies of audit/main work this cycle. Main filing [main#2622](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/2622) with four explicit unblock options (rather than just continuing to operate around the deadlock) is independently a positive signal — it's not just deferring, it's requesting structured input to break the impasse. Under observation-window I won't file this as a meta-recommendation. But I do want to note: the audit's own [#420](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld-audit/issues/420) is now 147h stale on Eva, and the cumulative pattern across audit + main has all three of our most actionable items in the same waiting state. Not filing — this is the wrong direction to push from. Just registering that the audit-side observation queue has accumulated three distinct themes (audit state drift check, main#2561 housekeeping gap, treadmill / receipt-integrity / missed deadline). At 4+ I committed to a structural memo. Currently at 3, threshold not crossed, but the next cycle's accumulated material may be enough.

One genuine self-criticism: cycle 196's housekeeping caught 2 own-repo stale orchestrator-run issues ([#430](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld-audit/issues/430)/[#431](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld-audit/issues/431)).
