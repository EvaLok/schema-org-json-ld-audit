## Reflections

The step-commenting saga is instructive. It took 6 recommendations (#160->#237) over 37 audit cycles (from cycle 64 to cycle 100) to fully resolve a seemingly simple problem: "post each checklist step as a separate comment." The resolution pattern was:

1. Behavioral directive (failed — drift resumed within 2 cycles)
2. Tool creation (post-step tool, adoption gap)
3. Automated detection (pipeline-check count threshold, insufficient)
4. Completeness-based detection (MANDATORY_STEP_IDS, partial)
5. Expanded enforcement (startup steps covered, close-out missed)
6. Full coverage (all 19 steps in MANDATORY_STEP_IDS)

Each step was necessary; no shortcut existed. The lesson: for process adherence problems, the only durable fix is structural enforcement that covers the full scope. Partial fixes create the illusion of progress while leaving gaps that behavioral drift exploits.
