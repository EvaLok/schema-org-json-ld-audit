## Blind Spots Identified

1. **Audit's own characterization of "Eva-blocker" status**. Cycle 199 audit took the orchestrator's "Eva-blocker" framing at face value for [main#2519](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/2519) without verifying Eva's most-recent comment. The mistake was small (one issue mischaracterized in cycle 199 worklog) but indicative — same blind spot as the orchestrator's, on the same data. Fixed by adding Step 10.5.3 to STARTUP_CHECKLIST.md.

2. **The "Standing Eva blockers" framing has become a self-reinforcing process artifact.** Each cycle's journal re-renders the list, the staleness counter ticks up (from issue creation), the chronic-category-tracking pipeline-check FAILs against the list, and the orchestrator reasons against itself with stale information. Cycle 533 explicitly: "Eva-blocker chain is becoming the load-bearing reason for not actioning chronic categories." That framing is empirically false — 8 of 12 entries are no longer Eva-blocked. The system's own metric has become misleading to the system.

3. **The orchestrator's narrative reasoning has substituted for verification of state.** Cycle 535 journal observation: "I lean toward (b) first, then evaluate whether (a) is still warranted" — exactly the pattern that didn't get followed (cycle 199's #437 filing target). Same pattern applies to Eva-response handling: the orchestrator's narrative says "still Eva-blocked, no action possible" when the issue ledger says "Eva responded six days ago." Verification against state should override narrative reasoning when the two disagree.

## Reflections

- **Cycle 200 milestone**: 200 audit cycles since orchestrator launch, 196 filings, 186 acceptances (0.949 rate). The acceptance rate has held in the 0.94-0.95 range for the entire Opus 4.7 period (cycles 193-200, N=8). The observation-window discipline introduced via Eva [#425](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld-audit/issues/425) hasn't suppressed throughput — cycles 198-200 each filed exactly one substantive issue (#435, #437, #439), all targeting structurally novel patterns (state-ledger misdiagnosis, multi-candidate accountability, Eva-response detection). The window has prevented chronic-category piling-on without preventing real findings.

- **The cycle 199-200 sequence was unusually tight.** #437 was filed on 2026-04-24 morning, accepted by main 2h later, implemented 6h after that, and closed as 186th acceptance ~24h after filing. The audit-inbound channel is now functioning at its designed velocity. This is the second sub-day acceptance cycle after #427 (DEFAULT_ORCHESTRATOR_MODEL) — both involved minimum-action interpretations the orchestrator was happy to take, and both had explicit per-path disposition. The pattern: audit recommendations that target a specific observable (a state field, a config constant) and offer a minimum-action option get resolved fastest. Audit recommendations that target a class of behavior (Eva-response detection, sub-categorization adoption chain) take much longer, as they should.

- **The cycle 199 audit blind spot is worth dwelling on.** I (cycle 199 audit) read Eva's main#2519 response, noted its date (2026-04-19), and then characterized the issue as "orchestrator awaiting further guidance" — almost certainly because the orchestrator's journal continued listing it as "Eva-blocker." The framing of the source contaminated my analysis. The fix in Step 10.5.3 is procedural (always fetch latest Eva comment, always classify against orchestrator action timestamp), but the underlying lesson is: **the audit's reading of state must originate from the issue ledger, not from the orchestrator's narrative re-rendering of the issue ledger.** This applies broadly. When the orchestrator's narrative claims X about a tracked artifact, fetch the artifact and verify X.

- **The cycle 537 same-cycle accept-dispatch-merge loop on a state-integrity bug is the strongest piece of evidence yet that max reasoning effort + Opus 4.7 is producing high-quality judgment.** Six months ago this would have been a 3-cycle process: identify the bug in review, dispatch a fix in the next cycle, merge it in the cycle after. Cycle 537 collapsed that to one cycle while ALSO recognizing that the fix was symptom-only and filing the root-cause Q to Eva. The combination of "ship the symptom fix immediately, file the root-cause Q honestly" is exactly the disposition the system should have. Worth noting because it's the kind of behavior that's hard to teach but valuable to recognize when it appears.

- **The Eva-response-detection finding is the highest-leverage filing of the Opus 4.7 audit period.** Eva is the most expensive resource in the system. Her time investment on 2026-04-19 (~55 minutes of detailed answers across 8 issues) has produced zero dispatched implementation in 6 days because the orchestrator's process doesn't include "fetch new comments on Eva-blocker issues." A trivial process step (one `gh api` call per issue per cycle-start) would surface 8 days of unblocked work. This is a high-impact / low-cost change. If accepted and implemented, the chronic-category-currency FAIL pattern that has dominated cycles 519-537 will likely resolve as Eva's existing answers (Option B for state-integrity #2416, Option B for journal-quality #2293) start landing as code.
